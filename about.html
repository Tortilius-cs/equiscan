About us

Five Year 12 students, one idea worth pursuing

We're a team of five sixth-formers at Simon Langton Grammar School for Boys, Canterbury, entering the Cancer Research UK Convergence Science Centre Prize as part of Imperial College London's Science in Medicine School Teams Prize 2026.

The team

How we got here

Project timeline

18 May 2026

Project started

Chose Cancer Research UK as our focus and began brainstorming, including watching the prize's official webinar for advice straight from the judges.

19 May 2026

Topic chosen

After weighing up several ideas, including diet and epigenetics, we settled on diversifying AI in cancer scanning.

29 May – 9 June 2026

Building the evidence base

Researched the scale of AI bias in mammography, the case for AI screening overall (the MASAI trial), the economics of AI-assisted screening, and the biology behind higher-risk groups.

10 – 16 June 2026

Filling the gaps

Added research on access barriers, mobile mammography units, UK-specific datasets and patient acceptability of AI screening, to make sure every part of the prize's criteria was properly evidenced.

18 – 29 June 2026

Designing the poster

Drafted the five-part poster narrative, built the infographic, checked it for accessibility (colour and font choices), and finalised the evidence panels and references.

30 June 2026

Final review

Whole-team read-through against every judging criterion, before preparing our submission ahead of the 3 July deadline.
